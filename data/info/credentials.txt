The Karyavattom Campus is the main residential and research hub of the University of Kerala. Located about 14 km from Thiruvananthapuram city, this is where the majority of the university's teaching departments (especially Science, Social Sciences, and Humanities) and research centers are located.

Here are the specific achievements, rankings, and highlights of the campus.

1. Rankings & Accreditation
The University of Kerala (centered largely around the research output from Karyavattom) has consistently performed well in national and international rankings.

NAAC Accreditation:
Grade: A++ (The highest possible grade)
Significance: It was the first university in Kerala to achieve the A++ grade, placing it among the top-tier institutions in India.

NIRF Rankings (National Institutional Ranking Framework):

2025 (State Public Universities): Ranked 5th in India.
2025 (University Category): Ranked 25th in India.
2025 (Overall): Ranked 42nd in India.

Note: The university has consistently maintained a position within the top 50 universities in India for several years.

QS World Rankings:
Asia University Rankings 2025: Ranked 339th in Asia.
Southern Asia Rank: 88th.

Campus Research & Facilities
Since Karyavattom is the research engine of the university, these facilities are central to its status:

SICC (Sophisticated Instrumentation and Computation Centre): A state-of-the-art facility providing high-end analytical instruments (like SEM, XRD, NMR) to researchers from the university and outside.

Technopark Proximity: The campus is located right next to Technopark (India's first IT park), fostering a unique industry-academia environment.

Green Campus: The campus is known for its lush greenery, the "Hymavathi Pond," and rich biodiversity, serving as a natural laboratory for its Botany and Zoology departments.
